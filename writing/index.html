Do Machine Learning Artefacts Have Politics?

Philosophy of technology·Autonomy, alignment, and emergent political orientation in LLMs

Extending Langdon Winner's 1980 thesis to large language models. Argues that machine learning artefacts cannot be apolitical: autonomy shifts technology from passive instrument to active co-principal, and the alignment process embeds emergent political orientation through persona selection. Proposes a linear model of an artefact's political influence, supported by an empirical LLM debate simulation.
